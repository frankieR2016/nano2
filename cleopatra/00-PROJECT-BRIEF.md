# The Last Pharaoh — Project Brief

**Title:** The Last Pharaoh
**Subtitle:** A Life of Cleopatra
**Length:** 15 story pages + cover + back cover (17 images)
**Aspect Ratio:** Vertical 2:3 for all pages
**Audience:** Francisco (9), Sebastian (7) — but written so adults engage too

## Theme
A woman who ruled by performance — and discovers, at the end, what's left when the audience is gone.

## Story Summary
Egypt 51–30 BCE. Cleopatra VII inherits a fading kingdom and a Roman shadow. She invents herself as a queen — through language, theater, charm — and uses two Roman generals (Caesar, then Antony) to keep Egypt alive. When Octavian wins, the performance fails. She chooses her own death over a Roman triumph.

## Three-Act Structure
- **Act 1 (Pages 1–4):** The Performer Takes the Stage. Exile, the bedroll prank, Caesar.
- **Act 2 (Pages 5–10):** Power, Love, and the Inimitable Livers. Caesarion, Caesar's death, the barge, Antony, the pearl, Actium.
- **Act 3 (Pages 11–15):** What's Left When the Audience Is Gone. Antony's death, Octavian, the choice, the asp.

## Anti-Drift Watch List
- **Page 7 (Barge on Cydnus):** Risk of fantasy-illustration drift. Anchor to "cinematic painterly realism, grounded photographic quality."
- **Page 8 (Inimitable Livers):** Risk of cartoonish drift from comedic beat. Keep the painterly anchor, candle-lit Alexandrian streets.
- **Page 10 (Actium):** Risk of textbook/educational drift. Specify "narrative cinematic painting, not illustration."
- **Page 14 (Dressing as Isis):** Risk of poster-art / sentimental drift. Ground in physical detail — mirror, oil lamps, linen.
- **Page 15 (The asp):** Risk of melodrama. Restraint. No blood. Calm hand. Lighthouse through window.

## Style Reinforcement Line (inject if drift)
"Cinematic painterly realism, grounded historical detail, restrained brush texture, mature graphic novel composition, NOT illustration, NOT cartoon."
