# Characters — The Last Pharaoh

## Cleopatra VII
Mid-20s through late 30s across the book. Mediterranean-Greek features (Macedonian Ptolemaic descent), olive skin, dark intelligent eyes, dark hair often in melon coiffure or braided with gold beads. NOT a conventional Hollywood beauty — face is striking, alert, slightly aquiline nose, strong jaw. Signature: gold cobra (uraeus) diadem when ruling, Isis robes for ceremony, simple linen sheath in private. Carries herself like a performer who is always aware of the room.

## Julius Caesar
Early 50s. Roman patrician. Lean, balding, sharp grey eyes, deep frown lines, laurel crown over thinning hair. Red-trimmed white toga or military cuirass. Wry mouth — a man who is amused at the world.

## Mark Antony
Late 30s to mid-40s. Roman general. Powerfully built, thick neck, curly dark hair, beard stubble, broad open face. Bronze cuirass over crimson tunic in war scenes; loose Greek chiton in Alexandria. Looks like a man who drinks and laughs loudly.

## Octavian (later Augustus)
Early 30s. Cool, pale, slight build, sandy hair, ice-blue eyes, an unreadable face. White toga with purple stripe. Looks young but radiates calculation. Never smiles in this book.

## Caesarion
A boy, ages 6-15 across the book. Mix of Cleopatra's features and Caesar's lean Roman build. Dark curls, watchful eyes, simple gold circlet.
